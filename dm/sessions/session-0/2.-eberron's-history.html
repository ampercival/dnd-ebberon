1. Eberron's History

Creation

The Dawn of Creation

Long before the rise of mortals or the first breath of history, the world of Eberron was shaped by forces as old as existence itself. According to myth, three Progenitor Dragons—Siberys, Eberron, and Khyber—wove the fabric of the planes, bringing the universe into being. Their creation was a perfect balance of light and shadow, order and chaos, life and death. But harmony was fleeting. Khyber, driven by ambition and darkness, betrayed Siberys, striking him down and scattering his golden essence across the sky as the Ring of Siberys. To contain this evil, Eberron coiled herself around Khyber, imprisoning the dark force within her coils and becoming the very world itself—the living, breathing planet you now call home. Thus, the Dragon Above, the Dragon Between, and the Dragon Below define the very essence of Eberron, their legacy etched into the land, sky, and the shadows beneath.

Though many cultures consider this story a myth, it resonates across the world, with Siberys embodying the sky and magic, Eberron as the life-giving earth, and Khyber as the dark underworld, filled with aberrations and fiends. The tale of their cosmic struggle remains a foundation of Eberron’s lore, with its influence still felt today in the world’s magic, natural forces, and the terrifying creatures that dwell deep below.

Ancient History (Over 100,000 to 5,000 years ago)

The rise and fall of great civilizations mark the mortal history of Eberron.

The Age of Demons

Over 100,000 years ago, Eberron was ruled by the dark children of Khyber—fiends, aberrations, and monsters that rose from the depths. Among them were the Overlords, thirty immortal archfiends who shaped the world to their malevolent desires. For millennia, these fiends reigned supreme, bending reality itself to their will. No civilization could stand against them.

But the tide of this dark era shifted when the Sovereign Host, along with an alliance of dragons and the celestial couatl, rose to challenge the overlords. The war that followed saw untold sacrifice, with the couatl binding the archfiends into eternal prisons using their own life force, creating the Silver Flame, a power that endures to this day. Though the overlords are bound, their influence still lingers, and those who seek to free them—like the Lords of Dust—work in the shadows, ensuring that the struggle against these ancient evils never truly ends.

The Age of Giants (80,000–40,000 years ago)

Around 80,000 years ago, the giants of Xen'drik rose to power, unlocking secrets of arcane magic that still surpass modern understanding. In their prime, these giant civilizations achieved incredible feats, binding elementals and creating eldritch machines of immense power. But their reach extended too far. When a war broke out between the giants and the quori of Dal Quor, the giants ended the conflict by destroying one of Eberron's moons, severing Dal Quor's link to the world.

This victory came at a terrible cost. Uprisings followed, most notably by the elves, who had long been enslaved by the giants. As the giants prepared to unleash catastrophic magic to crush the rebellion, the dragons intervened, emerging from Argonnessen to shatter the once-great civilizations of Xen'drik. They laid powerful curses upon the land, ensuring that no great empire could ever rise again. These draconic curses—such as the Traveler's Curse and Du’rashka Tul—warp space, twist minds, and keep Xen'drik a land of perilous, ever-shifting wilderness.

The Age of Monsters (40,000–5,000 years ago)

The Age of Monsters was dominated by the rise and fall of mighty civilizations on Khorvaire, especially the goblinoid Empire of Dhakaan, which flourished for over ten thousand years. From around 16,000 years ago, Dhakaan spread across central Khorvaire, pushing other races—dwarves, dragonborn, and orcs—to the continent's margins. But it wasn’t the only power shaping the land. After fleeing the destruction of Xen'drik, elves settled on Aerenal, around 25,000 years ago, forming the mystical Undying Court and the warrior tradition of the Tairnadal.

The Dhakaani Empire faced its greatest challenge around 9,000 years ago, with the invasion of the daelkyr, who led armies of aberrations from the plane of Xoriat. Though the Gatekeeper druids and Dhakaani champions ultimately sealed the daelkyr in Khyber, the damage was done. Madness crept through the goblinoid ranks, eroding the unity of their civilization. By 5,000 years ago, the empire crumbled into ruin and civil war, leaving behind scattered remnants of their once-great culture.

Today, Dhakaani ruins lie beneath many modern cities, and adventurers often explore these forgotten halls. The daelkyr’s influence persists as well, manifesting in the Cults of the Dragon Below and the Gatekeeper druids who continue to guard against aberrant threats. This ancient history still shapes the present, as goblins and orcs in places like Darguun seek to reclaim the glory of their fallen empire, while dwarves in the Mror Holds unearth strange relics from the lost Realm Below.

The Age Of Humanity (Last 5000 Years)

In the wake of the Age of Monsters, a new era began across Khorvaire—one that saw the rise of humanity and the discovery of the first dragonmarks. Scholars debate whether this era should be called the Age of Humanity or the Dragonmarked Age, as both human civilizations and the dragonmarked houses came into power. This era saw humanity spread from Sarlona to Khorvaire, where they established powerful city-states. During this time, the foundation for the Five Nations was laid, marking the early history of Galifar and the dominance of humans over the continent.

The Start of the Age (5000 to 1500 years ago)

Key events include the arrival of human settlers led by the adventurer Lhazaar, who established the Lhazaar Principalities around 3,000 years ago. Simultaneously, the first dragonmarks began to manifest, including the Mark of Hospitality among the halflings of the Talenta Plains and the Mark of Death among the elves of Aerenal. The elves soon faced tragedy as the Undying Court and dragons from Argonnessen annihilated the House of Vol, bearers of the Mark of Death, due to their dangerous experiments in combining elven and draconic magic.

By 2,500 years ago, human city-states like Korth, Metrol, and Wroat had risen to prominence, marking the early stages of what would become the Five Nations of Galifar. The discovery of dragonmarks continued, with the Mark of Making appearing in Metrol, and the Mark of Warding among the dwarves of the Ironroot Mountains. Giants and goblinoid civilizations receded further, their power diminished by human expansion. This growth led to conflicts, such as Karrn the Conqueror's failed attempts to unify Khorvaire under Karrnath, establishing centuries-long power struggles.

This time also saw the emergence of new races, including the kalashtar in Adar, formed by monks who voluntarily bound their spirits to the quori, creating a powerful new lineage. New cultures, such as the Venomous Demesne in modern Droaam, emerged from refugee tiefling and human settlements escaping Sarlona’s upheavals.

The War of the Mark (Approximately 1,500 years ago)

The War of the Mark was a dark chapter in Eberron’s history, marking the near-extinction of those with aberrant dragonmarks—strange and dangerous powers feared by the populace. Around 1,500 years ago, when the dragonmarked houses joined together as the Twelve, they turned their influence against those with aberrant marks. Propaganda stoked fear, casting the aberrant-marked as uncontrollable threats to society. Though many were innocent, they became scapegoats in a purge led by House Deneith soldiers, Vadalis trackers, and Medani inquisitors.

The war culminated in the rise of powerful champions like Halas Tarkanan and the Lady of the Plague, who made their final stand in Sharn, declaring it a haven for the persecuted. When defeat became inevitable, Tarkanan used his mark to shatter the city's towers, while the Lady unleashed diseases and vermin, destroying both the city and their enemies. Sharn lay in ruins for centuries afterward, a testament to the war’s devastation.

Though the aberrant marks were nearly eradicated, fear and prejudice endured. In recent years, aberrant marks have begun to reappear with growing frequency and power, raising concerns of another purge. Today, the secretive House Tarkanan trains those with aberrant marks, preparing for the possibility of a renewed conflict with the Twelve.

The Kingdom of Galifar (1 YK – 894 YK)

The Kingdom of Galifar stands as a golden age in the history of Khorvaire, an era of peace, progress, and unity. Founded in 1 YK by Galifar Wynarn, the kingdom unified the Five Nations not only through military might but also through diplomacy and a vision of a better world. Under Galifar’s rule, slavery was abolished, justice was reformed, and public education flourished, paving the way for the rise of the merchant class and economic stability. The Arcane Congress, Rekkenmark Academy, and other key institutions were established to serve the common good, fostering an age of advancement.

However, Galifar's reign wasn't without flaws. His policy of allowing the dragonmarked houses to dominate certain industries planted the seeds of their immense modern power, leading to near-monopolies that the Korth Edicts only partially curtailed. Additionally, his precarious system of succession—where his children ruled as governors of the Five Nations—sowed the seeds of discord. While this kept the realm stable for nearly nine centuries, it led to numerous rebellions and secessions over the kingdom's history, culminating in the Last War.

Despite its eventual downfall in 894 YK, the legacy of Galifar endures. The common laws, institutions, and cultural norms shared across the Five Nations are all remnants of that united past. Even after a century of brutal conflict, many still yearn for the golden age of Galifar and believe that true peace and unity can only be achieved by restoring the kingdom.

The Silver Crusade (830 – 882 YK)

The Silver Crusade began as a response to a rising tide of lycanthropy that threatened the western regions of Aundair. Once considered a rare and fearful legend, lycanthropy suddenly grew more virulent and dangerous during the early ninth century, with werewolf attacks and wererat infestations spreading fear and chaos. By 832 YK, the Keeper of the Flame, Jolan Sol, declared lycanthropy a corrupting force that needed to be eradicated, leading the Church of the Silver Flame to launch a brutal fifty-year campaign against the lycanthropes. The first two decades were particularly bloody as templars fought a losing battle against growing hordes of lycanthropes, many of whom were thought to be driven by the influence of a near-freed overlord. However, through decades of conflict, the templars eventually broke the power of the curse, weakening lycanthropy across Khorvaire.

Though the templars succeeded in weakening the lycanthropic threat, the Silver Crusade did not end without controversy. The final thirty years saw an ongoing inquisition led by a zealous sect known as the Pure Flame. They sought to purge every last lycanthrope but also targeted innocent shifters and other suspected werecreatures. The Pure Flame's actions created lasting rifts between the followers of the Silver Flame and the shifter communities, particularly in the Eldeen Reaches. While the Silver Crusade began as an effort to protect the people, it ended as a dark chapter of paranoia and hatred.

The Crusade's aftermath left a lasting mark on Eberron, with lycanthropy now treated as a serious public health risk. Templars of the Silver Flame are still charged with dealing with lycanthropes, though they often seek to cure rather than kill, except when necessary. Meanwhile, lycanthropes have found havens in places like Droaam and Stormreach, and the scars of the crusade still affect shifters across the continent.

The Last War (894 YK – 996 YK)

The Last War was a century-long conflict that shattered the once-united kingdom of Galifar and forever altered the landscape of Khorvaire. After the death of King Jarot in 894 YK, a bitter struggle for succession erupted between his children, plunging the Five Nations into open war. What began as a civil war soon spiraled into a vast and brutal conflict, marked by shifting alliances, political intrigue, and grueling battles. Magic and military innovation fueled the war, giving rise to devastating new weapons and war machines, such as the warforged, constructs created to serve as soldiers in the endless conflict.

The war reached its darkest moment on 20 Olarune 994 YK with the Day of Mourning, when the nation of Cyre was obliterated by a mysterious magical cataclysm. The Mourning left behind the Mournland, a twisted wasteland filled with living spells and undead horrors, and claimed over a million lives in a single day. The shock of the Mourning forced the warring nations to the negotiating table, and the conflict came to a fragile end with the signing of the Treaty of Thronehold in 996 YK. Although the war is over, no one claims victory, and many believe that it is only a matter of time before hostilities reignite.

Post-Last War (996 YK – Present)

Though the Treaty of Thronehold brought a formal end to the Last War, Khorvaire remains a continent on edge. The scars of the war are everywhere—cities lie in ruins, forests and farmlands are scorched, and refugees from Cyre wander the land, homeless and destitute. The Mourning haunts the continent, with many fearing that its destructive power could return or be harnessed as a weapon. This fear keeps the fragile peace in place, but few believe that it will last.

Nations continue to jockey for position, eyeing their neighbors with suspicion while secretly rebuilding their military forces. The rise of new powers, such as the Valenar elves and the goblins of Darguun, further complicates the political landscape. Meanwhile, the dragonmarked houses, having profited enormously during the war, now wield unprecedented power, controlling vital industries such as transportation, banking, and healing. The warforged, once mere tools of war, now walk free, struggling to find their place in a world that once needed them only for battle.

As of 998 YK, two years after the war's end, the continent stands at a crossroads. Peace is fragile, and every nation is making preparations for the possibility of future conflict. The mystery of the Mourning looms large, and while some seek to understand its origins, others prepare for its potential return. The Last War may be over, but its shadow lingers across Eberron, and the wounds of the past are far from healed.
