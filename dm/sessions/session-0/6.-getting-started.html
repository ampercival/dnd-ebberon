1. Getting Started

To kick off our Eberron campaign, we’ll be using a two-part "Session Zero" to lay the foundation for both the story and your characters. These sessions will help set clear expectations, make sure everyone’s comfortable with the gameplay, and give you everything you need to create characters that feel right at home in the world of Eberron.

Here’s how it will work:

  • In the first session, we’ll go over the campaign guidelines, dive into the unique lore of Eberron, and start discussing your character concepts.
  • Afterward, you’ll have a bit of homework—fleshing out your backstory, figuring out your character’s goals, and thinking about how they fit into the group.
  • When we meet for the second session, we’ll finalize your characters, introduce the starting hook for the campaign, and make sure everyone is ready to jump into the adventure.

Session 0a: Laying the Foundation

What We’ll Cover

  • Establish Guidelines and Expectations

    • Set overall campaign rules and group expectations for gameplay, boundaries, and conduct.
    • Establish a schedule

  • Introduction to Eberron

    • Overview of Eberron’s unique setting, including its rich lore, history, diverse locations, and inhabitants.
    • Overview of how your characters will begin in the campaign.

  • Character Creation Discussion

    • Explore the key aspects of your character’s backstory.
    • Collaborate and discuss potential character concepts and how they may interact in the story.

Desired Outcomes

  • Everyone understands the group's expectations, boundaries, rules, and dynamics.
  • Players have a foundational understanding of Eberron to begin shaping their characters.
  • The group has an open discussion about character ideas and possible party roles.
  • By the end of the session, players should have chosen their class and species, and have the groundwork to begin developing their backstory.

Considerations

Party Composition and Class Selection

When choosing your class, it's important to select something that excites you while ensuring the group has a balanced mix of roles. A well-rounded party can handle various challenges more effectively. Discuss with your group to make sure there’s a good spread of abilities, whether it's tanking, healing, sneaking, or dealing massive damage. While it's not necessary to fill every role perfectly, having a diverse set of skills helps cover all the bases and makes the group more adaptable to different situations.

Consider these categories:
- Tank/Front-Line Fighter: Absorbs damage and holds enemies back (Barbarian, Fighter, Paladin).
- Healer/Support: Keeps the party alive and buffs allies (Cleric, Druid, Bard).
- Explorer/Investigator: Specializes in sneaking, traps, and finding secrets (Rogue, Ranger).
- Magic-User/Glass Cannon: Deals high damage with spells but is fragile (Wizard, Sorcerer, Warlock).
- Face: Excels in social encounters and negotiation (Bard, Paladin, Sorcerer).

Starting Equipment

DM Rule

DO NOT select any starting equipment. You will have gold from both your class and your background. Record this on your player sheet for now.

You MAY select a starting trinket.

Dragonmarks

DM Rule

Your characters are NOT dragonmarked

Homework: Develop Backstories

What you'll do

For homework, players will develop their character backstories by exploring the following elements:

  • Choosing Your Class: Select the class that defines your character’s abilities and role in the world.
  • Choosing Your Species: Choose a species that shapes your character’s culture, history, and worldview.
  • Choosing Your Alignment: Decide on an alignment that reflects your character’s moral compass while fitting with the group dynamics.
  • Exploring Faith and Beliefs: Determine the role of religion or philosophy in your character’s life and their connection to Eberron’s many belief systems.
  • Your Role in the Last War: Reflect on how the Last War affected your character and their journey after the conflict.
  • Selecting Or Creating a Background: Build or select a background that summarizes your character’s experiences and the skills they’ve gained.
  • Establishing Character Goals: Set long-term, mid-term, and short-term goals that will drive your character’s actions and decisions.
  • Determining Connections with Other Characters: Explore potential relationships and shared histories with other party members to create a cohesive group.

Desired Outcomes

  • Players have had time to thoughtfully develop their backstories and characters.
  • Players are ready to discuss and share their character concepts with the group at the next session.
  • Players are excited and eager to start the campaign with a strong understanding of their character’s motivations and role in the story.

Session 0b: Finalizing and Getting Started

What We'll Do

  • Introduce Your Character: Share the backstory you’ve developed, highlighting the key points that define who your character is, their motivations, and why they are part of this adventure.
  • Determine Ability Scores: We’ll finalize your character’s ability scores using one of the methods outlined in the 2024 Player’s Handbook—either standard array, point buy, or rolling.
  • Get the Hook: We’ll introduce the campaign’s starting hook, setting the stage for how the characters come together and begin their shared adventure.
  • Fill in the Details: Together, we’ll complete your character sheets by selecting equipment, filling in any missing features, and finalizing the last touches that bring your character to life.

Considerations

  • Ensure all players are comfortable with their characters' abilities, background, and place within the group dynamic.
  • Double-check that everyone’s character goals and connections align with the overall story.
  • Ensure each player understands how their character fits into the opening of the campaign and how they might interact with the rest of the party.
