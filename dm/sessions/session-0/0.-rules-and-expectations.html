1. Rules and Expectations

Ground Rules

Respect Each Other and Communicate Openly
  • We come together to have fun and share a great adventure.
  • Treat everyone with respect and kindness.
  • Be considerate of each other's feelings and perspectives.
  • Communicate openly with the DM and fellow players.
  • If you have any concerns or ideas, please share them—constructive feedback helps improve the game for everyone.
Consent, Comfort, and Inclusivity
  • We aim to create a safe and inclusive environment for all participants.
  • If any content or situation makes you uncomfortable, let the group or the DM know immediately.
  • We'll make adjustments to ensure everyone feels comfortable and included.
Be Punctual and Prepared
  • Our sessions are scheduled at specific times.
  • Please be punctual and ready to play at the agreed-upon time.
  • If you can't make it to a session, inform the group in advance.
Stay Engaged
  • Pay attention and stay engaged in the game.
  • Avoid distractions like mobile phones or side conversations during the session.
  • Immerse yourself in the story and contribute actively to the adventure.
Avoid Metagaming
  • Remember to separate your knowledge as a player from your character's knowledge.
  • Let your character discover information organically within the game world.
  • This maintains the integrity and excitement of the story.
Rules and Fair Play
  • Familiarize yourself with the basic rules of Dungeons & Dragons.
  • D&D is a collaborative storytelling experience; the goal is collective enjoyment rather than winning.
  • Play fair and respect the DM's rulings.
  • Don't hesitate to ask questions if you're unsure about something—it's okay to learn as you go.
Be Creative
  • Bring creativity to your character and actions.
  • Think outside the box and contribute to the narrative.
  • Your imagination is a key part of what makes the game exciting and memorable.
Conflict Resolution
  • If conflicts arise, whether in-character or out-of-character, address them calmly and respectfully.
  • In-character conflicts can add depth to the story but should not affect real-life relationships among players.
  • The DM is there to help mediate and resolve issues.
  • Keep the focus on having a positive and enjoyable experience for everyone.
Expectations for Preparation
  • Character Development: Invest time in developing your character's backstory and motivations. This enriches the game for everyone.
  • Session Readiness: Ensure you have all necessary materials ready before the session starts, including updated character sheets and any personal notes.
Respect the Game World
  • Respect the game world and the narrative crafted by the DM.
  • Make decisions that align with your character's personality and background.
  • Help build a cohesive and immersive story by engaging with the setting and other characters.

  2. Are there any additional ground rules you think we should include?
  3. Is everyone comfortable with the expectations we've set for the game?

Death and Resurrection

Death is a significant aspect of the game that can greatly impact the story and player experience. It's important to find a balance between understanding the risks and consequences of death and ensuring that players don't take excessive risks due to a lack of meaningful repercussions.

Some considerations include:

  • Let-the-Dice-Fall-Where-They-May: Embrace the risk of combat where, if an enemy lands a critical hit, characters might die—even in the first session. This approach underscores the seriousness of dangerous situations and encourages careful decision-making.
  • Unconsciousness at Zero Hit Points: Dropping to zero hit points renders a character unconscious but not immediately dead, allowing for recovery as long as someone survives. This reduces sudden character deaths while still highlighting the peril of combat.
  • Flexible Resurrection Rules: Make resurrection more accessible by adjusting requirements (e.g., the cleric doesn't need diamonds for revivify as long as they have the equivalent gold). This can ease the finality of death but may lessen the perceived risks if resurrection is too easy.
  • Lasting Scars or Injuries: Instead of death, a character who drops to zero hit points survives with a lasting scar or injury, adding depth to their story. This provides consequences without removing the character from the game, balancing risk and continuity.
  • Balancing Risk and Reward: Ensure that death has meaningful consequences to encourage thoughtful play. If consequences are too severe, players may become overly cautious; if too light, they may act recklessly.
DM Question

How should we handle character death and resurrection to balance the risks and consequences in our game?

Considerations:

  • Maintaining meaningful consequences for risky actions.
  • Avoiding overly harsh penalties that discourage engagement.
  • Preventing reckless behavior due to negligible consequences.
  • Keeping the game fun and the story impactful.

Options:

  • Use standard rules: death saving throws and resurrection spells as written.
  • Characters become unconscious at zero hit points, with death occurring under specific conditions.
  • Allow for easier resurrection but introduce costs or side effects to maintain stakes.
  • Implement lasting scars or injuries as consequences of dropping to zero hit points.

DMing Style

Story-Driven Approach

As your DM, I prioritize storytelling and narrative flow. The rules of Dungeons & Dragons serve as a framework, but I may choose to ignore or override a rule in a particular scene to enhance the story or maintain pacing.

  • Rules as Guidelines: While I respect the rules, I may make on-the-spot decisions to keep the game moving smoothly.
  • Open to Input: If you notice a rule that might have been overlooked, please bring it to my attention. Often, it may be an oversight.
  • Final Decisions: If I acknowledge a rule and decide to intentionally ignore it for narrative reasons, I'd appreciate if we can proceed without further debate during the session.
DM Question

Are you comfortable with a story-driven approach where rules might be adjusted for narrative purposes?

Balancing Rules and Narrative

While storytelling is a priority, the rules are still important for fairness and consistency.

  • Rule Discussions: If there's a disagreement about a rule, we can briefly discuss it, but I may make a final decision to keep the game moving.
  • Post-Session Clarifications: For complex rule questions, we can delve deeper after the session to find a solution that works for everyone.

Are you agreeable to addressing detailed rule discussions after the session to maintain game flow?

Lines and Veils

Lines and Veils are tools used to establish boundaries within our game to ensure that all players feel comfortable and safe. They help define which topics are acceptable, which should be approached with discretion, and which should be avoided entirely.

  • Lines are hard boundaries—subjects that players do not want to be included in the game under any circumstances. These topics should not be brought up at all, even in passing.

  • Veils are softer boundaries—subjects that can be part of the story but are kept in soft focus or occur off-camera. These elements might be mentioned or implied but are not described in detail.

It's the difference between "this doesn't come up at all" and "this may be a part of the plot but treated with some discretion."

Understanding each other's Lines and Veils allows us to create a respectful and enjoyable gaming experience for everyone. It's important to discuss these boundaries openly and honestly.

Examples:

  • Line Example: A player may state, "I don't want cruelty to animals to feature in this game at all, even as a scene-setting detail." This means the DM and other players should avoid introducing this element entirely.

  • Veil Example: A player may state, "I'd be OK with someone mentioning a horse looking overworked or a dog looking neglected and hungry to show how bad things are but I don't want a scene of the villain kicking puppies specifically." This means the DM and other players be careful with how they approach the topic.

Even after setting initial Lines and Veils, it's crucial to remain open to further discussions. Sometimes, you might not realize a topic is uncomfortable until it arises during gameplay.

  3. What is your preferred way of addressing new boundaries that might arise during gameplay?
