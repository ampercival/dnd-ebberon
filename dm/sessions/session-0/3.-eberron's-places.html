1. Eberron's Places

The World

https://eberronmap.johnarcadian.com/

The world of Eberron is vast, diverse, and full of wonders, spanning multiple continents, each with its own distinct cultures, peoples, and dangers.

  • Khorvaire: The starting stage of adventures is Khorvaire, the largest and most populous continent. It is a land rich in history, industry, and intrigue—home to towering cities, ancient ruins, and the scars of war. Khorvaire is the heart of the Five Nations, and it is where the Last War unfolded, reshaping the continent forever. Once the unified Kingdom of Galifar, this continent is now a fractured land of independent nations. From the sprawling metropolis of Sharn, the City of Towers, to the icy plains of Karrnath, the lightning rail routes of Breland, and the magical academies of Aundair, Khorvaire is the center of politics, trade, and conflict in Eberron.
  • Xen'drik: Southwest of Khorvaire lies Xen'drik, a vast jungle continent filled with ancient ruins and the remnants of the once-great giant civilizations. This land is shrouded in mystery and danger. Adventurers come here seeking treasure, lost magic, and the hidden secrets of the giant empire, but they must contend with the wild, unpredictable magic of the land, and the deadly creatures that call Xen'drik home.
  • Aerenal: South of Khorvaire lies Aerenal, an island continent ruled by the immortal elves. Here, the elves worship their undying ancestors, who guide their society from beyond death. The island is steeped in ancient magic, with the elves guarding the arcane secrets they’ve accumulated over millennia. Aerenal remains isolated from much of the world, its people focused inward, guarding their ancient rites and secrets.
  • Argonnessen: Far to the southeast, Argonnessen is the land of dragons, a place forbidden to most mortals. Here, dragons dwell in ancient cities, studying the Draconic Prophecy and guarding secrets that could reshape the world. Few who travel to Argonnessen ever return, and those who do are often forever changed by what they’ve seen.
  • Sarlona: Across the eastern sea lies the continent of Sarlona, the original birthplace of humanity. Though once home to a multitude of kingdoms, today, the continent is largely dominated by the Inspired—psionic rulers of the Riedran Empire, who command armies of psychic warriors and enforce strict control over their people. Beneath the surface, the Kalashtar—a race of psionically gifted humans bound to spiritual entities called quori—quietly wage a resistance against the Inspired.

Khorvaire: The Heart of Eberron

Khorvaire is the continent where most adventures in Eberron begin—a land rich in history, magic, and intrigue. Thanks to the tireless efforts of House Sivis and House Orien, Khorvaire is well-mapped and connected, making it the most navigable of Eberron’s continents. However, even though many regions are well-known, there are still uncharted corners filled with ancient secrets, wild dangers, and mysteries waiting to be uncovered.

General Overview

Humans make up the majority of Khorvaire’s population, but this diverse continent is home to a wide array of cultures, races, and creatures. Once united under the Kingdom of Galifar, Khorvaire is now divided into various nations, some as old as history itself, others forged in the crucible of the Last War. While peace reigns for now, the scars of the war are still fresh, and tensions simmer just beneath the surface.

Khorvaire’s Nations are connected by a well-maintained system of roads, along with the lightning rail of House Orien that spans the central nations, allowing swift travel between major cities. For those with deeper pockets, the airships of House Lyrandar offer the fastest, albeit most expensive, means of travel. Whether by land, rail, or air, Khorvaire’s network of transportation keeps its fractured nations tied together, though rivalries and suspicions remain strong.

Life in Khorvaire

Everyday life in Khorvaire reflects the continent’s complexity. The lingering effects of the Last War, the dominance of the dragonmarked houses, and the omnipresence of magic all shape the culture of its nations.

Language and Culture

Khorvaire's vast population is multilingual, with Common serving as the trade language and most widely spoken tongue. However, regional languages like Dwarvish in the Mror Holds, Giant in Xen'drik, and Goblin in Darguun and the Shadow Marches reflect the deep history of the continent. The naming conventions of Khorvaire also vary by region and culture, with nobles adding the "ir'" prefix to their family names, and dragonmarked heirs using the "d'" prefix to signify their status.

Calendar and Time

The common calendar of Khorvaire is based on the twelve moons of Eberron, with each month corresponding to the closest moon. The timeline begins with the foundation of the Kingdom of Galifar, measured in YK (Years of the Kingdom). By default, most campaigns in Khorvaire begin in the year 998 YK, just two years after the Treaty of Thronehold ended the Last War, though the continent remains on edge.

Currency

Trade and commerce in Khorvaire are built on a stable system of currency established during the Galifar era. Coins such as the gold galifar (gp), silver sovereign (sp), and copper crown (cp) are still used across the nations, ensuring a standardized economy. Wealthy merchants and nobles often use letters of credit backed by the bankers of the Mror Holds, while common folk still use coins for day-to-day transactions.

The Countries

Let’s explore each country a little more, moving from west to east, though not in any particular order of importance. Keep in mind that the original empire, Galifar, which was shattered by the Last War, consisted of five core nations: Aundair, Thrane, Breland, Cyre, and Karrnath. These are still referred to as the Five Nations, and their legacy continues to shape the politics and culture of Khorvaire today.

Shadow Marches

The Shadow Marches are a desolate, swampy region filled with moors and mystery, home to the largest half-orc population in Khorvaire. Though not a formal nation, the Marches are primarily known for their rich deposits of Eberron dragonshards, making them a hub for prospectors. The orcs and humans here live in isolated tribes, with a long history tied to the ancient struggle against the daelkyr, aberrant creatures who left behind madness and twisted monsters in the swamps. House Tharashk, a half-orc house known for the Mark of Finding, dominates the region's commerce through Zarash’ak, their unofficial capital. The Shadow Marches are also the birthplace of the Gatekeepers, a druidic sect that continues to protect the land from extraplanar threats, though dangerous cults and daelkyr relics still linger in the shadows.

Droaam

A monstrous nation ruled by the Daughters of Sora Kell, Droaam is a land where creatures like ogres, trolls, harpies, gnolls, and medusas thrive. Formed just over a decade ago, this wild territory west of the Graywall Mountains has quickly gained strength and stability under the hags' leadership, despite not being recognized by the Treaty of Thronehold. Its capital, the Great Crag, is growing, along with the frontier cities like Graywall. House Tharashk partners with Droaam to export monstrous mercenaries and the rare byeshk ore. Though most of Khorvaire dismisses Droaam as a temporary alliance, the nation's power continues to rise, making the rest of the continent uneasy.

Demon Wastes

The Demon Wastes is a desolate and dangerous region, where rivers of lava cut through blackened plains and volcanic glass. Once home to powerful fiends and ancient demon lords, this cursed land is now inhabited by fiends, including devils, rakshasas, and demons, as well as the brutal Carrion Tribes, who worship these dark powers. The only barrier keeping these evils from spreading is the Labyrinth, a series of canyons patrolled by the Ghaash'kala, a tribe of orcs sworn to contain the horrors of the Wastes. Though its ruins may hold ancient treasures, the Demon Wastes remain a deadly and treacherous place where few dare to venture.

Eldeen Reaches

The Eldeen Reaches are a vast, loosely governed region of fertile farmlands and the untamed Towering Wood. Once part of Aundair, the farmers of eastern Aundair seceded during the Last War with the help of druids and rangers, forming their own nation. The Reaches are home to a diverse population, including humans, shifters, and nomadic druidic circles. The Wardens of the Wood, the largest druid sect, maintain peace and harmony, guided by the Great Druid, an awakened greatpine named Oalian. Known for agriculture and animal husbandry, the Reaches blend natural magic into everyday life, where druids advise communities, and awakened beasts and fey creatures are part of the society. Although peaceful, the Reachers remain wary of Aundair and prepare for a possible conflict to defend their independence.

Aundair

Aundair is a kingdom steeped in magic, ruled by the ambitious Queen Aurala ir’Wynarn. Known for its arcane prowess, Aundair is home to the prestigious magical academy of Arcanix, and the floating towers that symbolize its mastery over wizardry. The majority of its population consists of humans, with half-elves and elves often found among the nation’s scholars and nobles. Dwarves and gnomes also contribute to the kingdom’s vibrant academic and artistic communities. Aundairians value knowledge, finesse, and fashion, and their land is known for producing fine wines, cheeses, and grain. Despite its small size, Aundair held its own in the Last War through superior magical power. However, tensions linger with both Thrane and the Eldeen Reaches, and many Aundairians still seek to reclaim lost territories. Magic permeates everyday life, and Aundair's elegant culture stands in stark contrast to the scars left by the war.

Thrane

Thrane is a human theocracy ruled by the Church of the Silver Flame, with 11-year-old Jaela Daran as the divine Keeper of the Flame. The Silver Flame’s purpose is to defend the innocent from supernatural threats, making Thrane a beacon of faith and righteousness. While the faith is central, not all Thranes uphold its ideals—zealots seek to unite Khorvaire under the Flame, while corrupt priests pursue power. The nation is home to many clerics and paladins, though magewrights and wizards are less common. Thrane’s deep faith unites its people, but its strict adherence to the Silver Flame also fosters distrust toward necromancers, warlocks, and practitioners of dark magic.

Breland

Ruled by the popular King Boranel, Breland is a powerful and industrialized nation, known for its pragmatism, innovation, and intrigue. It boasts one of the most diverse populations in Khorvaire, with humans, half-elves, and halflings making up the majority, while its cities—especially Sharn—also welcome a variety of monstrous races, including goblins, ogres, orcs, and even harpies and gargoyles from nearby Droaam. Breland's large population and vast resources make it a hub of industry and espionage, but also a hotbed for organized crime. The nation played a significant role in the Last War and is home to the largest number of Cyran refugees. Though King Boranel remains beloved, some citizens advocate for a shift toward democracy once he passes, leaving Breland’s future uncertain.

Zilargo

Zilargo is the gnomish homeland, a peaceful-seeming paradise known for its brilliant minds, elemental binding, and fine alchemy. The Library of Korranberg, the largest repository of knowledge in Khorvaire, and the Korranberg Chronicle, the continent’s most popular newspaper, are based here. Despite the idyllic appearance, Zil society is a web of intrigue, with every gnome seemingly involved in one scheme or another. The Trust, a ruthless secret police force, eliminates threats to society with preemptive efficiency, ensuring the streets remain safe. As long as one follows the unwritten rules of Zil intrigue, life in Zilargo is serene—but crossing the Trust is a fatal mistake.

Darguun

Darguun is a volatile and young nation ruled by the hobgoblin leader Lhesh Haruuc Sharaat'kor. Once part of Cyre, Darguun was seized during the Last War by the goblinoid mercenaries who had fought for the Five Nations. Now home to goblins, hobgoblins, and bugbears, the country is known for its fierce warriors and mercenary forces. While recognized as a nation under the Treaty of Thronehold, Darguun operates on its own terms, and its laws differ greatly from those of other treaty nations. Mercenaries, adventurers, and traders may find opportunities in this land, but it remains dangerous and unpredictable.

The Mournland (formerly Cyre)

Once the shining heart of the Kingdom of Galifar, Cyre was obliterated by a magical cataclysm known as the Mourning. Now, the Mournland is a desolate wasteland of twisted magic and ghostly ruins, where wounds never heal and the dead do not decompose. Mutated monsters, living spells, and other strange dangers roam this cursed land, which remains largely uninhabited save for some warforged factions, including the enigmatic Lord of Blades. The survivors of Cyre, scattered across Khorvaire, dream of rebuilding their lost nation, though many simply strive to survive in a world that views them with a mix of pity and suspicion.

Karrnath

Karrnath is a grim and disciplined human kingdom ruled by King Kaius III, known for its military prowess and strict laws. During the Last War, the nation famously used undead troops—skeletons and zombies—raised by the necromancers of the Blood of Vol to bolster their armies. While the undead still serve as laborers and guards, Kaius has distanced himself from the Blood of Vol, creating tension between its followers and traditionalists who see the use of undead as a stain on Karrnath's honor. The people of Karrnath, predominantly humans with smaller populations of dwarves and half-elves, are tough and resilient, used to enduring harsh winters and strict rationing. Military training is a source of great pride, and the Rekkenmark Academy remains the premier institute for military officers. Though the war has ended, many Karrns still believe they would have won had it continued, and the nation remains on high alert, wary of unrest from within and without.

Talenta Plains

The Talenta Plains are home to the nomadic halflings, known for their close bond with the dinosaurs they ride and raise. These wild plains are vast, filled with roaming dinosaur herds and the halfling tribes that live off the land, following the guidance of their chieftains and shamans. Life here revolves around hunting, tradition, and a deep reverence for ancestral and natural spirits. While the halflings united under Lathon Halpum during the Last War to defend their way of life, they now face challenges as more outsiders cross the Plains, bringing both trade and conflict. Despite being recognized as a nation under the Treaty of Thronehold, the halflings must now decide if they will embrace change or push back against the encroaching influences.

Valenar

Valenar is a land claimed by the warrior elves of Tairnadal during the Last War. Known for their unmatched cavalry and devotion to the arts of war, the Valenar elves took the region from Cyre, invoking ancient claims to the land. Their kingdom is primarily composed of open plains ideal for breeding their famous horses, with harsher environments like the Blade Desert serving as natural borders. Valenar elves live for battle and adventure, frequently patrolling their land in warbands or seeking challenges abroad. The daily affairs of the kingdom are managed by half-elves, while Cyran humans, the former inhabitants of the region, continue their lives under new rulers. Though Valenar is now a recognized sovereign nation, the elves remain focused on their next conflict, pushing the limits of peace as they seek worthy opponents.

Q'barra

A rugged frontier on the edge of Khorvaire, Q'barra was initially settled by those seeking refuge from the Last War, including loyalists to the old Galifar Empire and Cyran refugees. Its dense jungles and untamed wilderness hide both danger and opportunity. While human settlements are few, the region is rich with deposits of Eberron dragonshards, attracting prospectors, fortune-seekers, and criminals alike. Beneath the surface, however, Q'barra is home to ancient civilizations of lizardfolk and dragonborn, including the Cold Sun Federation and the hostile Poison Dusk tribes. Tensions between the settlers and the indigenous "scales" continue to rise, particularly as treaties are violated and settlers push deeper into lands tied to fiendish powers from the Age of Demons.

Mror Holds

The mountainous home of the dwarves, the Mror Holds is a land of wealth and industry. Known for its vast metal mines and advanced metalwork, the dwarves of Mror have a reputation as master merchants and craftsmen. After discovering ancient dwarven ruins deep within the Ironroot Mountains, the clans uncovered riches and powerful artifacts, but also awakened an ancient evil: the daelkyr lord Dyrrn the Corruptor. Now, the dwarves battle aberrations in the depths of the Realm Below while continuing to mine its treasures. The Holds are a loose confederation of twelve noble clans, each governing a hold, but the threat of the daelkyr and differing views on how to deal with the aberrations—some clans embracing their power, others shunning it—threatens their unity.

Lhazaar Principalities

The Lhazaar Principalities are a loose alliance of pirate lords, merchant princes, and sea barons who control the northeastern coastline of Khorvaire. Recognized as a nation under the Treaty of Thronehold, each island domain operates independently, often feuding with one another. The Lhazaar are the best sailors in Khorvaire, serving as privateers during the Last War and continuing piracy alongside legitimate merchant trade. Leadership is fluid, with anyone able to rise from sailor to prince through cunning and strength. High Prince Ryger ir'Wynarn of Regalport seeks to unite the principalities under his rule, but most princes value their independence. The Lhazaar Principalities also have a significant changeling population and a number of foundlings with the Mark of Storm, who are unaffiliated with House Lyrandar but seek to acquire airships for their own gain.
