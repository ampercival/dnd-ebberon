Hook

The Korranberg Chronicle newsroom buzzes with the frantic energy of a city that never sleeps. Quills scratch feverishly across parchment, the steady clacking of arcane typewriters creating a constant rhythm. The scent of warm Zilargo coffee wafts through the room, mingling with the sharp tang of fresh ink and the faint ozone of everbright lanterns flickering overhead.

The newsroom itself is a chaotic mosaic of movement—runners darting between desks, laden with hastily scrawled messages and half-rolled scrolls, while editors bark orders over the din, their voices sharp and cutting like the crack of a whip. Each desk is cluttered with piles of parchment, crumpled drafts, and arcane devices—small enchanted stones humming with the magic of distant correspondents reporting from the far corners of Khorvaire. Scribes transcribe messages sent through speaking stones, while arcane bells ring with incoming news from across the continent.

Eyes follow as you and a few other unfamiliar faces are led by a discreet and efficient warforged assistant, its brass-plated body gleaming in the everbright light as it ushers you forward with polite but firm gesture to the office of Thalia ir'Zannath, the Chronicle’s sharp-eyed and stern editor. Each of you was summoned, quite unexpectedly, by a terse message from Thalia herself.

aside: The prefix "ir'" in her name, a subtle yet telling marker of nobility in Eberron. The "ir'" denotes that she hails from a noble family, part of the old bloodlines that still hold sway in the Five Nations. Though nobility in Eberron doesn't always equate to vast power or riches, it often comes with influence and a sense of duty to one's house. Thalia, however, seems more focused on her role at the Chronicle than on leveraging her title, choosing to wield her influence through stories and uncovering the truth rather than through courtly politics. Keep in mind, her noble background may also afford her connections and resources that a common editor wouldn’t have.

You push past a blur of faces all flushed with the thrill of the next big scoop. Somewhere near the back, a dwarven journalist can be heard grumbling about a botched story from the Mror Holds, while a gnome hurriedly sketches a political cartoon that depicts a scandal among the Dragonmarked Houses. The Chronicle is a living, breathing beast, hungry for stories, and you can feel its pulse in the very walls of the building.

As you reach Thalia ir'Zannath's office, the noise fades—though the energy doesn’t.
Her office is a cluttered sanctuary in the storm. The half-elf editor sits behind a desk overflowing with old editions of the Chronicle, rolled-up maps, and notes scribbled on everything from parchment scraps to napkins. A great iron globe of Eberron hangs suspended in the air beside her, rotating slowly, its surface etched with pins marking stories in distant lands. One wall is dominated by a towering bookshelf, crammed with dusty tomes, journals, and research materials, all barely held together by a tangle of red ribbons used for cross-referencing. Across from it, a large, detailed map of Khorvaire sprawls across the wall, covered in ink marks and hastily pinned notes marking key locations in the city. It's a testament to Thalia's obsession with finding stories in every corner of the city of towers.

She thanks the warforged with a curt nod as it quietly slips out of the room. "Close the door," she says, her voice carrying the same authority that keeps the newsroom in check. She gestures for you all to sit as she leans forward, fingers tapping thoughtfully on the desk.

“You’re probably wondering why I called you here—and why you’re sitting with people you’ve never met,” Thalia begins, her gaze shifting between you. “We don’t usually throw our reporters into danger, but you’re not just reporters, are you?”

She pauses, letting the tension hang in the air for a moment before continuing. “The Chronicle has a knack for recognizing talent, for finding those with something...extra. In a world as dangerous as Eberron, we need more than just sharp minds and quick pens—we need people with innate abilities, people who can handle more than just words.” Her eyes narrow, her voice growing more intense. “People like you.”

She continues, "Eberron is a world on edge, still haunted by the lingering tensions of the Last War. While most people go about their lives, unaware of the dangers lurking beneath the surface, there are some—like you—who are different. Who have the power to face those dangers head-on. From the fanatics of the Emerald Claw to the cults of the Dragon Below, to the far-reaching plots of the daelkyr and ancient archfiends, Eberron is a world in need of heroes. And the Chronicle? Well, the Chronicle knows where to find them."

Thalia leans forward, her voice taking on a tone both earnest and knowing as she pulls a thick dossier from her desk and places it gently before you. Her sharp green eyes scan the room, pausing on each of you with an intensity that feels less like an appraisal and more like a recognition. "You’ve been selected to join the The Lantern Bearers, a special group here at the Chronicle. A group who sheds light on the hidden truths of the world, guiding the way like a beacon of hope and knowledge--a group of our most elite" she says, her voice steady and sincere. "The Chronicle has always been more than just a newspaper. We don’t just report the news—we uncover the truth, no matter the cost. And some stories... well, some stories are too dangerous, too extraordinary, for ordinary reporters."

She taps the dossier, her expression softening slightly as she continues. "That’s why we created the The Lanterns Bearer—a special task force of individuals with the talent and drive to not only report on the world’s most mysterious and dangerous events but to face them head-on. You see, the Chronicle has been watching you since you first walked through our doors. We've seen your courage, your instincts, your unique abilities. We've known, all along, that you had something more to offer. That's why you’re here today."

She lets her words sink in for a moment before continuing, leaning in just a little closer. "The Lantern Bearers aren’t just journalists—they’re investigators, explorers, protectors. They go where others won’t and tackle stories that could change the course of nations—or reveal secrets that some would rather stay buried. But the Lantern Bearers don’t work alone. When a crisis or mystery demands it, we form what we call a Dispatch—a group of Lantern Bearers brought together to take on the most dangerous assignments, to protect the truth and make sure the wrong hands never grasp it. Today, you are that Dispatch."

Thalia leans back, her gaze warm yet firm. "I’m not here to force you into anything. But understand this: the Chronicle doesn’t pick its Lantern Bearers lightly. You’ve been watched because we believe in you, because we’ve seen that spark in you that’s needed to make a difference in this world. This is your chance to not only write history but to protect it. And as part of this Dispatch, you’ll be standing on the front lines of the stories that matter most."

She offers a small, knowing smile. "So the question isn’t whether you’re ready—you’ve been ready longer than you know. The question is whether you're willing to take this step, to embrace the role the Chronicle believes you were meant for."
