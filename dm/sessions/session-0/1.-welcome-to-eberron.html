1. Welcome to Eberron

Introduction

Welcome to Eberron, a world of magic woven into the fabric of daily life where airships soar between towered cities, lightning rails race across continents, and elemental power fuels industry and innovation. But this is also a land scarred by a century of war, where fragile truces barely hold the nations together, and shadows of the Last War loom large. Intrigue, danger, and moral ambiguity define the world, where dragonmarked dynasties hold power rivaling nations, and nothing is ever as simple as it seems. This is a place where adventure awaits, and your choices will shape the future.

Let's explore what makes Eberron a unique setting for our adventure

Eberron is Magic is woven into everyday life

In Eberron, magic isn't something reserved for mysterious wizards or rare moments of wonder. No, here magic powers the world itself. Picture this: vast cities where the streets are lit not by mundane flames, but by everbright lanterns—arcane lights that never flicker, never fade. Look up, and you’ll see airships drifting lazily between towers that scrape the sky, their bound elementals crackling with energy. Beneath your feet, lightning rails zip across the continent, powered by elemental forces as passengers travel at speeds undreamed of elsewhere. This is Eberron—where magic isn’t just something you study, it’s something you live with every day.

But this isn’t your typical fantasy magic—it’s an integral part of life, almost like technology in our world. Magewrights, specialists in small but essential spells, use magic to craft fine items, maintain city infrastructure, and even breed magical animals. In the forges of House Cannith, ancient magic is combined with ingenuity to produce wonders like the warforged—living constructs made of wood and steel, given life and sentience to serve as soldiers during the Last War.

In Eberron, magic shapes everything. It builds the cities, fuels the economy, and created beings like the warforged, who are still searching for their place in a world that once needed them for destruction but now must find a way to live with them in peace. Magic, in Eberron, is both a tool for progress and a force of disruption. It’s everywhere, in everything, and it’s waiting for you to tap into its wonders—or its dangers.

Eberron is DND with a Twist

In Eberron, nothing is quite as it seems. Here, the lines between good and evil blur, and the world is steeped in moral ambiguity. Imagine a place where the most noble hero might be forced to make dark, questionable decisions, and even the brightest lights cast the deepest shadows. This is a world of intrigue and noir, where corruption festers beneath the surface and powerful organizations pull the strings behind the scenes. In Eberron, good and evil aren’t just black and white—they’re shaped by culture, circumstance, and personal ambition.

One of the most striking differences in Eberron is that traditional alignments don’t always apply in the way you might expect. That gold dragon you meet? It may not be the benevolent sage you're used to. It could have its own selfish or even destructive agenda. The orc you encounter might be a paragon of law and virtue, and the drow lurking in the shadows may be no more dangerous than the humans from Aundair. In Eberron, morality is fluid. A person’s actions are not defined by the color of their skin or the shape of their body, but by their choices, their experiences, and their ambitions.

That doesn’t mean evil doesn’t exist—it’s everywhere. But the evil you’ll encounter in Eberron isn’t always a cackling villain twirling a mustache. Instead, it might be the kindly innkeeper who shorts her customers to pad her own pocket, or the guard who takes bribes to let criminals through the gate. You might even meet a crusading paladin of the Silver Flame—someone who fights for righteousness—but who crosses lines, like zealously hunting down heretics with no regard for collateral damage.

The reality is that evil in Eberron often looks like selfishness, greed, and a willingness to harm others for personal gain. The mage who sacrifices innocents in the name of a cure, the spy who tortures for information—these are people who see their actions as necessary, even righteous. In this world, your decisions may not just be about right and wrong—they may be about what you’re willing to live with, or who you’re willing to trust in a place where trust is a rare and precious thing.

So, as you explore Eberron, remember that the classic rules don’t always apply. Fiends might offer genuine help, dragons may pursue their own agendas, and allies could become your greatest enemies. Your choices will carry weight, and the consequences will ripple across the world in ways you never expected. In Eberron, nothing is ever simple—and that’s what makes it so captivating.

Eberron is Pulp Adventure

Eberron is a world where high adventure collides with a sense of constant danger. It’s a place where you’ll leap from the deck of an airship as it crashes in flames, chase down enemies on the speeding lightning rail, and battle mad cultists in the shadowy depths of ancient ruins. It’s a world that embraces the excitement and action of pulp fiction—filled with bold heroes, sinister villains, and impossible odds.

Imagine the sprawling jungles of Xen'drik, a continent teeming with mysteries from lost civilizations. Here, dark elves ride giant scorpions and hurl metal boomerangs with deadly precision, while deep within, forgotten ruins hide treasures and powers beyond imagining. Or perhaps you find yourself racing across Khorvaire on the lightning rail, your foes closing in as the train rockets toward its destination, suspended above the ground by arcs of elemental energy. And above it all, airships powered by bound elementals sail across the sky—if you’re lucky, you’ll end up fighting on one of these, and if you’re really lucky, you’ll survive.

But it’s not just about exotic locations and thrilling action. Eberron is home to mad cultists plotting to unleash the chaos of the Dragon Below, crazy wizards studying the shifting planes as they await the right celestial conjunction to power their infernal machines, and more than a few shadowy organizations all vying for world domination. Everywhere you go, there’s the potential for high-stakes encounters, grand spectacles, and dangerous schemes.

At the heart of it all is the Draconic Prophecy—a cosmic puzzle woven into the fabric of the world itself. Dragons, immortals, and fiends all seek to interpret its secrets, believing they can shape the future to their will. But the Prophecy doesn’t predict a single destiny. It shows countless possible futures, all shaped by the choices of mortals like you. Every action, every decision, could shift the balance of power in ways you can’t yet imagine.

In Eberron, you’re not just an adventurer—you’re a hero in a world teetering on the edge. Your strength, skill, and courage will be tested at every turn, but so will your wits and your heart. Whether you’re leaping from crashing airships, battling evil cults in forgotten ruins, or outwitting ancient dragons, every moment in Eberron is filled with excitement, danger, and the chance to make your mark on a world where nothing is ever quite what it seems.

And remember—remarkable heroes often find themselves up against equally remarkable villains. You’ll face rivals who match your strength, villains who return after seeming death with vengeance on their minds, and shadowy figures whose plans stretch across entire nations. Every victory will push your foes to grow stronger, forcing you to rise to meet the challenge. Welcome to the pulp adventure of Eberron, where the stakes are always high, and the only way out is through.

Eberron is Dragonmakred Dynasties

In Eberron, true power lies not just in crowns and swords, but in the dragonmarked houses—ancient dynasties whose bloodlines bear magical sigils known as dragonmarks. These marks aren’t tattoos; they’re living magic, granting unique abilities to those lucky enough to manifest them. Imagine heirs with the Mark of Healing, raising the dead for House Jorasco, or airship pilots of House Lyrandar, controlling the skies with the Mark of Storms.

These houses run like powerful corporations, monopolizing industries across Khorvaire. If you need healing, travel, or banking, you’ll deal with one of these houses. Their influence rivals that of entire nations, and their reach is everywhere—from cities to wilderness. Even independent businesses often bear their approval, showing the sheer control they hold over commerce.

Dragonmarks appear during adolescence, but not every heir receives one. Those who do gain prestige, adding the 'd’ prefix to their names, becoming part of an elite force that shapes Eberron’s future. Yet the dragonmarked houses are filled with intrigue—some heirs are exiled, called excoriates, while foundlings possess marks without the backing of the powerful families.

Once kept in check by the Korth Edicts, the houses’ power has only grown since the Last War. Now, they push the limits of their influence, with some wondering if they’ll ever be stopped. For adventurers like you, the dragonmarked houses offer both opportunity and danger. Aligning with them could bring wealth and power, but crossing them could mean your downfall.

The marks and their houses are,

  • House Medani (Mark of Detection): Masters of uncovering hidden threats, specializing in risk assessment, protection, and community service over power.

  • House Tharashk (Mark of Finding): Experts in tracking, bounty hunting, and dragonshard prospecting, driven by the unified strength of human and half-orc clans.

  • House Vadalis (Mark of Handling): Masters of breeding and training animals, from mundane beasts to magical creatures like hippogriffs, with rumors of even more ambitious magebreeding experiments.

  • House Jorasco (Mark of Healing): Renowned healers who can cure the sick and even pull souls back from death—at a price—while rumors persist of darker experiments conducted during the Last War.

  • House Ghallanda (Mark of Hospitality): Masters of comfort and connection, Ghallanda halflings run inns, taverns, and sanctuaries across the Five Nations, offering both hospitality and quiet influence through a network of bound favors and well-placed allies.

  • House Cannith (Mark of Making): Masters of creation, House Cannith controls the manufacture of weapons, warforged, and magical goods, but the house is fractured—three rival barons vie for leadership after the Mourning devastated their ancestral seat in Cyre.

  • House Orien (Mark of Passage): Masters of transportation, House Orien controls the lightning rail and overland caravans, but faces growing challenges from the Mournland's destruction and the rise of Lyrandar airships.

  • House Sivis (Mark of Scribing): House Sivis, led by gnomes, masters communication—managing speaking stones and training scribes and interpreters. Neutral and discreet, they facilitate long-distance messages and notarize important documents with their arcane marks.

  • House Deneith (Mark of Sentinel): House Deneith, born of war, offers elite mercenaries, bodyguards, and law-enforcing Sentinel Marshals. Neutral yet powerful, they serve all sides while guarding their honor and keeping Khorvaire safe from danger.

  • The Houses of Shadow (Mark of Shadow): House Phiarlan and House Thuranni excel in illusion and espionage, wielding the Mark of Shadow to deceive, entertain, and assassinate. Though split by a bitter feud, the houses remain unmatched as spies and artists, sculpting secrets from the shadows of Khorvaire.

  • House Lyrandar (Mark of Storm): Masters of wind and sea, House Lyrandar uses the Mark of Storm to command weather and elemental vessels, ruling both maritime trade and the skies with their airships, while their island enclave, Stormhome, stands as their sanctuary and seat of power.

  • House Kundarak (Mark of Warding): Guardians of wealth and security, House Kundarak wields the Mark of Warding to protect valuables with mystic locks and wards, operating both a vast banking network and the infamous Dreadhold prison, renowned for its legendary security and extradimensional vaults.

Eberron is Scarred by the Last War

The Last War may have ended, but its shadow looms large over Eberron. For a century, the Five Nations fought a brutal conflict that shattered the kingdom of Galifar. Though the Treaty of Thronehold declared peace, it’s a fragile one, held together by fear and uncertainty. The Mourning—an inexplicable cataclysm that obliterated Cyre in a single day—serves as a chilling reminder of the devastation that unchecked power can wreak. The land of Cyre is now a twisted wasteland known as the Mournland, a scar on the continent of Khorvaire, haunted by undead and living spells that still roam the battlefield.

The atmosphere in Eberron echoes the tensions of post-World War I Europe. Soldiers return home unsure of their place in a world at peace. Nations eye each other with suspicion, their leaders quietly preparing for the inevitable return of conflict. Magic-driven war machines and weapons of incredible power litter the landscape, remnants of a war that never really ended in the hearts and minds of the people.

The warforged, living constructs created for battle, now walk free—granted citizenship by the treaty that ended the war. But for many, peace is an uncomfortable fit. Warforged were designed for a world at war, and many struggle to find their place in a society that fears them as tools of destruction with no purpose beyond combat.

The Mourning stands at the heart of every conversation about the future. Was it an accident? A weapon gone wrong? Or something far worse, still waiting to be unleashed? Fear of the Mourning keeps the nations in check, but for how long? Everyone knows that peace in Eberron is temporary—the Last War may have paused, but few believe it’s truly over.

The world of Eberron is waiting for you—full of mystery, magic, and adventure. What will you make of it?
