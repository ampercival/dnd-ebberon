1. Eberron's People

Eberron is a diverse place where many different races coexist, each bringing unique cultures, histories, and abilities to the world. From the bustling cities of Khorvaire to the untamed frontiers, every race has carved out its place in the aftermath of the Last War. With the rise of dragonmarked houses and the ancient struggles of long-lost empires, the people of Eberron are shaped by their history and their roles in this complex and magical world.

Races in Eberron

Most of the races in Eberron have similarities to their standard counterparts in D&D, but there are notable differences that give them a unique flavor. Additionally, many races are tied to specific dragonmarks, which grant them special powers and influence over various industries and guilds across the continent.

Humans

Humans are the most common race in Eberron and are found in the majority of its kingdoms. They are versatile and ambitious, with some bearing dragonmarks that grant them great power. Humans can have the Mark of Finding, Mark of Handling, Mark of Making, Mark of Passage, or Mark of Sentinel, and they dominate most political, social, and economic landscapes, especially in the Five Nations.

Half-Elves

In Eberron, half-elves are known as the Khoravar, a distinct race with their own culture. While they have some similarities to humans and elves, they stand apart with their unique identities. Half-elves can bear the Mark of Detection or the Mark of Storm, and many have positions of influence within their dragonmarked houses or serve as intermediaries between different cultures.

Half-Orcs

Half-orcs in Eberron trace their lineage back to ancient struggles with aberrant invaders from another plane. They often live in frontier areas, working as prospectors, trappers, or druids. Many have the Mark of Finding, which aids them in their search for dragonshards, valuable magical crystals scattered across Eberron.

Halflings

Halflings in Eberron fall into two distinct categories. There are city-dwelling halflings who often work as innkeepers, healers, or merchants, using the Mark of Healing or Mark of Hospitality to support their work. Then there are the nomadic halflings of the Talenta Plains, who tame and ride dinosaurs, living a more primal and tribal lifestyle as fierce hunters and ambushers.

Gnomes

Gnomes in Eberron are known for their cunning and mastery of elemental binding, a vital magical technology. They play key roles in banking, magic, and politics, though much of their influence is kept secret. Gnomes can bear the Mark of Scribing, which enhances their roles as scribes, chroniclers, and agents of subterfuge. Their homeland, Zilargo, is a place of intrigue, hidden power, and conspiracy.

Elves

There are three main groups of elves in Eberron. The Aereni elves on the island of Aerenal revere their ancestors and maintain ancient traditions, with some serving the mysterious Undying Court. The Valenar elves are warrior nomads who staked a claim on Khorvaire during the Last War, renowned for their cavalry and martial skill. The Phiarlan and Thuranni elves bear the Mark of Shadow and run powerful guilds focusing on entertainment, espionage, and intrigue.

Dwarves

Dwarves in Eberron are master miners and craftsmen, living in powerful holds in the Mror Mountains. Their Mark of Warding allows them to excel in banking, defense, and protective magic. Dwarves also have a history of delving deep into the earth, uncovering both riches and ancient dangers, which have given rise to conflicts with powerful aberrations.

Changelings

Changelings are shapeshifters, descendants of doppelgangers and humans, who can alter their appearance at will. This makes them natural spies, infiltrators, and diplomats, though their ability to hide in plain sight often leads to suspicion. Changelings never bear dragonmarks but are valued for their versatility and adaptability.

Shifters

Shifters are the descendants of lycanthropes and humans, able to temporarily take on animalistic traits, such as growing claws, increasing speed, or heightening their senses. Shifters are often found in rural or wild areas, acting as rangers or hunters, and are closely tied to the natural world.

Warforged

Warforged are living constructs, originally created to serve as soldiers during the Last War. Now that the war is over, they seek purpose beyond the battlefield, struggling with questions of identity and freedom. As sentient beings made of metal and magic, warforged don't need to eat or sleep, but they face widespread prejudice despite being granted full citizenship after the Treaty of Thronehold.

Dragonborn

These dragonborn are believed to be descendants of an ancient empire and serve as guardians of their draconic heritage, with a deep connection to the Draconic Prophecy. Their society emphasizes honor, tradition, and service, and while they lack dragonmarks, their innate magic makes them powerful sorcerers and warriors. Some dragonborn venture out as mercenaries or explorers, seeking to unlock the secrets of their draconic legacy and protect the world from ancient threats.

Tieflings

Tieflings in Eberron descend from humans whose bloodlines were tainted by fiendish pacts, particularly those from the Venomous Demesne in Droaam, where ancient families fled the influence of demons and devils in Sarlona. While often viewed with suspicion due to their infernal ancestry, tieflings are not inherently evil, though their connection to dark magic is undeniable. Many tieflings become powerful sorcerers, warlocks, or rogues, using their fiendish heritage to gain power in a world that frequently distrusts them. Despite having no dragonmarks, their infernal bloodline grants them unique abilities, making them influential players in Eberron's politics and intrigue.

Demographics

The population demographics of Khorvaire are diverse, though exact numbers are difficult to determine. No official census has ever been conducted, and the upheaval caused by the Last War further complicated matters. Scholars, however, have been able to estimate the rough distribution of species across the continent, excluding the underdark region known as Khyber and other continents like Aerenal. These estimates reflect only the known populations on Khorvaire itself and highlight the presence of both common races and the more exotic inhabitants of the land.
